Getting experience as a psychology student

Build Experience While You Study

Experience does not need to be perfectly matched to your career plans to be valuable. The key is developing the skills employers look for — communication, research, empathy, analytical thinking, and professionalism — through whatever opportunities are available to you. Here are the most useful types of experience for psychology students specifically.

Research Experience

Research skills are one of psychology's strongest selling points with employers. Look for opportunities to use them beyond your coursework.

  • Apply for undergraduate research assistant roles within the School of Psychology
  • Participate in staff-led research projects — ask your lecturers directly
  • Choose an applied dissertation topic relevant to your career interests
  • Build data skills through online courses in SPSS, R, or Excel
People-Facing Experience

For psychology graduates, experience working with people is particularly valuable — it demonstrates the empathy, communication and professional boundaries employers expect.

  • Peer mentoring within Liverpool Hope — supports others while building your own skills
  • Volunteering with community or charity organisations
  • Support worker or care roles (part-time)
  • Coaching, tutoring, or youth work

Professional and Leadership Experience

Taking on responsibility within university life is one of the most efficient ways to build the leadership and teamwork skills employers value.

  • Student Ambassador Scheme — excellent for communication and professional development
  • Student Representative roles — demonstrates initiative and professional engagement
  • Psychology Society committee membership
  • BPS Student Membership — starts building your professional identity early

Work Placements

A placement year gives you a significant advantage in the graduate job market — employers consistently rate work experience as one of the most important factors in hiring decisions.

  • Liverpool Hope offers an optional placement year module
  • Placements are available across a range of sectors relevant to psychology
  • Even shorter summer internships make a meaningful difference

Support available to you

Get Careers Support

Liverpool Hope's careers service offers one-to-one support, employer events, and resources throughout your degree. As a psychology student, there are specific things worth knowing about how to use this support most effectively.

Psychology students have direct access to careers advisers with experience of psychology-specific routes. Whether you are interested in clinical training, HR, research or something you have not considered yet, a careers appointment is the best starting point. It is free, confidential, and available throughout your degree and for up to four years after graduation.

One-to-One Careers Appointments

Book a session with a careers adviser to discuss your options, get CV feedback, or prepare for applications and interviews. As a psychology student, it is worth asking specifically about:

  • Non-clinical careers your degree suits
  • Graduate schemes that recruit from psychology
  • How to talk about your skills to employers
  • Routes into further training if you are considering clinical, forensic or occupational psychology

What employers are looking for

Develop Your Skills

Employers consistently value psychology graduates for their research skills, communication, empathy and analytical thinking. What is shifting is the growing importance of digital confidence, commercial awareness and the ability to apply psychological knowledge outside traditional helping roles.

The most employable psychology graduates are those who can demonstrate their skills with evidence — specific examples from their degree, placements, volunteering or part-time work — rather than simply listing them. Start collecting evidence of your skills now, even if a career direction is not yet clear.

After you graduate

What Happens After Graduation

Your degree is a starting point, not a destination. Most psychology career paths begin with a graduate-entry role that builds experience — some routes require further training to reach chartered or registered practitioner status.

Graduate entry
Direct entry roles
Many careers in HR, research, marketing, public sector, charity and consultancy are open on graduation with no further training required.
Further training
Postgraduate routes
Clinical, forensic, occupational and sport psychology require a BPS-accredited Masters or doctorate. These are competitive — experience during your degree matters significantly.
Graduate entry
Graduate schemes
Many large employers run graduate schemes open to psychology graduates — NHS, Civil Service, retail, finance, consultancy and more. Most open in autumn of your final year.
Ongoing
Alumni support
Liverpool Hope's careers service is available to graduates for up to four years after completing your degree. You are not on your own after graduation.
