Employer Trends and Practical Support

What employers are looking for from psychology graduates in 2026, how career interests are shifting, and practical tools to help you prepare.

What employers want

What Employers Want in 2026

Employers are looking for psychology graduates who can combine strong people skills with adaptability, digital confidence and real-world experience. Across sectors, the ability to demonstrate your degree skills in practical contexts is increasingly important.

AI and Digital Roles
Critical thinking, data awareness, ethical judgement and understanding of human behaviour are increasingly valued.
Self-Employment Pathways
Communication, creativity, professionalism and independence are essential for freelance or entrepreneurial routes.
Helping Professions
Empathy, active listening and strong professional boundaries remain the core requirements in counselling and care roles.
Across All Sectors
Communication, research, problem-solving, teamwork and ethical awareness — and the ability to evidence them.

Practical support

Tools to Help You Prepare

Using LinkedIn

LinkedIn is one of the most useful tools for exploring career pathways, connecting with alumni and building a professional profile. Here's how to get started:

  • Set up a basic profile with your degree, university and a short summary of your interests
  • Follow Liverpool Hope University and organisations relevant to your career interests
  • Connect with psychology graduates to learn about their career paths
  • Use the Jobs section to explore entry-level and graduate roles
  • Engage with content from employers and professional bodies like the BPS
CV Support

Your psychology degree has given you a strong set of transferable skills. The challenge is knowing how to describe them clearly on a CV.

  • Frame research methods experience as data collection, analysis and report writing
  • Describe presentations and group projects as communication and teamwork evidence
  • Include part-time work, volunteering and student roles — they all count
  • Tailor your personal statement to each role you apply for
  • Contact Student Futures for one-to-one CV support and feedback
Placements and Volunteering

Experience does not need to be perfect to be valuable. Any role that involves working with people, gathering information or solving problems builds relevant skills.

  • Support work, care roles and charity volunteering build empathy and professional boundaries
  • Coaching, tutoring and mentoring demonstrate communication and leadership
  • Research assistant or lab helper roles build research experience
  • Student society roles, peer mentoring and rep positions show initiative
  • Check the Liverpool Hope Volunteering Hub for local opportunities
