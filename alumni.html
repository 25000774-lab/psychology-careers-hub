Sport and Exercise Psychologist
Dr Alexander Smith
Degree: BSc Sports Psychology Graduated: 2016 Current role: Sport and Exercise Psychologist, Liverpool F.C.

After graduating from Liverpool Hope University, I gained experience volunteering with local sports clubs before completing further training in Sport and Exercise Psychology. This helped me develop the practical experience and professional skills needed to work in elite sport.


I now work as a Sport and Exercise Psychologist at Liverpool F.C., supporting athletes, coaches and teams with performance, confidence, motivation and wellbeing. My role includes one-to-one sessions, group workshops and psychological skills training.


My degree helped me develop key skills in communication, research, understanding behaviour, ethical awareness and evidence-based practice. These skills are central to my work with athletes and support staff.

Advice to current students

Start gaining experience early, even through volunteering, coaching or supporting university sports teams. Sport psychology takes time and further training, but practical experience and strong communication skills are just as important as academic qualifications.

PHOTO
CBT Therapist
Dr Jasmine Perceval
Degree: BSc Psychology Graduated: 2017 Current role: Cognitive Behavioural Therapist

After graduating from Liverpool Hope University, I knew I was interested in mental health but was unsure which specific route I wanted to take. I began working in support roles, which helped me build confidence working directly with people and understand the importance of empathy, patience and professional boundaries.


I later completed further training in Cognitive Behavioural Therapy and now work as a CBT Therapist, supporting clients to understand the links between their thoughts, feelings and behaviours.


My psychology degree gave me a strong foundation in mental health, research evidence and reflective practice.

Advice to current students

Do not worry if your career path is not completely clear yet. Focus on gaining experience, learning from each opportunity, and understanding what kind of work suits you. Your first role after university can be a stepping stone, not your final destination.
